The roadmap · Coming soon

FinOps is the first lens. Four more are coming.

Every DynaLens app starts the same way — a real, recurring problem we watched Dynatrace teams solve by hand. Here's what each one fixes, and how.

SG
SignalComing soon
Incident intelligence
The problem

A Davis problem fires at 3am. The on-call engineer opens a wall of metrics, entities, and events — and burns the first ten minutes of the incident just working out what's actually broken and who needs to know.

Signal reads the problem the moment it opens and writes the brief for you: a plain-language summary of what happened, what it affects, and the most likely cause — routed to the right people before anyone has to ask.

What you get
  • Incident summaries generated from Davis problem cards
  • Audience-aware views: on-call detail, lead summary, exec rollup
  • Delivered to email, Teams, Slack, or your ITSM

FL
FlowComing soon
ITSM automation
The problem

Dynatrace sees the problem first — but the ticket still gets raised by hand. Someone copies the details into ServiceNow, someone else forgets to close it, and the audit trail between monitoring and resolution quietly falls apart.

Flow makes the link automatic and two-way: a problem opens a ticket, a resolution closes it, and every ticket carries the full Dynatrace context, timeline, and root cause with it.

What you get
  • Auto-created tickets in ServiceNow, Jira, or any ITSM
  • Auto-close on resolution, with timeline and root cause attached
  • Routing rules by severity, team ownership, and environment

MP
MapComing soon
Business-service mapping
The problem

Smartscape knows every host, process, and service — but the business asks "is payments healthy?", not "how is prod-eu-k8s-node-07?". Translating infrastructure topology into something a stakeholder understands is still a manual, out-of-date slide deck.

Map sits on top of Smartscape and lets you name reality: group the underlying entities into the business services people actually care about, then watch health, SLOs, and problems through that lens.

What you get
  • Business services composed from live Smartscape entities
  • Health, SLO status, and active problems in business terms
  • Shareable views for stakeholders without a Dynatrace seat

AG
AgentComing soon
AI operations teammate
The problem

The answers are all in Dynatrace — but they're locked behind DQL and dashboard fluency. So the same two people field every "what's going on with X?" question, and the rest of the team waits on them.

Agent opens that knowledge to everyone: ask your environment a question in plain English and get a grounded answer — during an incident, a review, or a routine check, no query language required.

What you get
  • Natural-language questions answered from live Dynatrace data
  • Incident context on demand: what failed, what changed, blast radius
  • Scheduled health checks, compliance, and capacity reviews
